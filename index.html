Enriching Dutch Case Law Markup

digitalheir
m.f.a.trompper@uva.nl

Abstract

A growing amount of Dutch case law is openly distributed on Rechtspraak.nl. Currently, many documents are not marked up or marked up only very sparsely, hampering our ability to process these documents automatically.

In this thesis, we explore the problem of automatically assigning a document structure to the texts of Dutch court judgments. To this end, we develop a database that mirrors the XML data offering of Rechtspraak.nl. We experiment with Linear-Chain Conditional Random Fields to label text elements with their role in the document (text, title or numbering). Afterwards, we experiment with stochastic context free grammars to generate a parse tree to model section nesting.
